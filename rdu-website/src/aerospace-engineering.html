Graduate Learning Outcomes

Graduates will be able to:

1

Aerodynamic Analysis

Analyze fluid flow and aerodynamic forces for aircraft and spacecraft design and optimization.

2

Structural Design

Design aerospace structures considering weight, strength, and performance requirements.

3

Propulsion Systems

Analyze and design propulsion systems for various aerospace applications and missions.

4

Flight Dynamics

Model and analyze vehicle motion, stability, and control characteristics for flight systems.

5

Space Systems

Design spacecraft and satellite systems for various orbital missions and space exploration.

6

Systems Integration

Integrate multiple aerospace subsystems while managing complexity and interdependencies.

7

Testing & Validation

Plan and conduct testing programs to validate aerospace system performance and safety.

8

Professional Practice

Apply industry standards, regulations, and professional ethics in aerospace engineering practice.

Laboratories & Facilities

Wind Tunnel Laboratory

Subsonic and supersonic wind tunnels for aerodynamic testing and flow visualization studies.

Propulsion Test Cell

Engine testing facilities with thrust measurement and performance analysis capabilities.

Flight Simulator

Advanced flight simulation systems for aircraft handling and control system development.

Design & Manufacturing

CAD/CAM systems, 3D printing, and composite manufacturing equipment for prototype development.

Capstone Design Examples

CubeSat Mission Design

Design and build a complete CubeSat for Earth observation or technology demonstration missions.

Electric Aircraft Design

Develop innovative electric propulsion systems for sustainable aviation applications.

Mars Rover Systems

Design robotic systems for planetary exploration including mobility and scientific instruments.

Hypersonic Vehicle Design

Analyze and design vehicles for hypersonic flight including thermal protection systems.
