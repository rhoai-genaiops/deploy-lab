Graduate Learning Outcomes

Graduates will be able to:

1

Epidemiological Analysis

Design and conduct epidemiological studies to investigate disease patterns and health outcomes in populations.

2

Statistical Methods

Apply biostatistical methods to analyze public health data and interpret research findings appropriately.

3

Health Program Planning

Develop, implement, and evaluate public health programs and interventions for diverse populations.

4

Policy Analysis

Analyze health policies and their impacts on population health outcomes and health equity.

5

Environmental Assessment

Assess environmental factors affecting human health and recommend mitigation strategies.

6

Community Engagement

Engage with diverse communities to address health disparities and promote health equity.

7

Global Health Perspective

Understand global health challenges and develop culturally appropriate health solutions.

8

Professional Leadership

Demonstrate leadership skills and ethical decision-making in public health practice.

Laboratories & Facilities

Epidemiology Laboratory

Computer lab with statistical software including SAS, SPSS, and R for epidemiological analysis.

Community Health Center

On-campus clinic providing practical experience in community health assessment and intervention.

Environmental Health Lab
