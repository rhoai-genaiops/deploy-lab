Critical Reading

Read & interpret complex texts and cultural artifacts across traditions and contexts.

2

Persuasive Communication

Argue persuasively in writing and speech with evidence, clarity, and ethical awareness.

3

Research Methods

Use methods from history, literature, philosophy, social sciences, and the arts to pose and answer research questions.

4

Data Analysis

Analyze data and quantitative information to support reasoning and decision-making.

5

Global Engagement

Engage globally with languages, cultures, and comparative perspectives.

6

Methods & Research Training

Qualitative Methods

Archival Research, Ethnography Studio, Textual Criticism, Performance Research, Practice-Based Research in the Arts.

Quantitative Methods

Statistical Modeling with R, Survey & Causal Inference, Data Visualization & Storytelling, Digital Analytics.

Digital Tools

Python/R for humanities, GIS mapping, digital editions & TEI, web development, database design.

Creative Methods

Studio practice, documentary production, exhibition curation, community-based participatory research.

Capstone Options

Research Thesis

40-60 page thesis under faculty supervision with defense & public colloquium, archived in institutional repository.

Creative/Digital Project

Exhibition, performance, film, or digital project with 8-12k word critical commentary and public presentation.

Civic Impact Project

Policy, education, or community partnership project with evaluation report & public dissemination.

Portfolio Integration

All tracks include reflective portfolio mapping learning outcomes to evidence across four years of study.
