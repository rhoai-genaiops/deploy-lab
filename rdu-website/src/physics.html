Graduate Learning Outcomes

Graduates will be able to:

1

Physical Principles

Apply fundamental physics principles to analyze and solve complex problems across all major areas of physics.

2

Experimental Skills

Design, conduct, and analyze sophisticated physics experiments using modern instrumentation and techniques.

3

Mathematical Modeling

Use advanced mathematics to model physical phenomena and interpret theoretical predictions.

4

Computational Methods

Employ computational tools and programming languages to solve physics problems and simulate systems.

5

Data Analysis

Process, analyze, and interpret experimental data using statistical methods and uncertainty analysis.

6

Scientific Communication

Communicate physics concepts and research findings effectively to diverse audiences through various media.

7

Research & Innovation

Conduct independent research projects and contribute to the advancement of physics knowledge.

8

Critical Thinking

Evaluate scientific literature, assess theoretical models, and make evidence-based conclusions.

Laboratories & Facilities

Teaching Laboratories

Modern physics labs with oscilloscopes, function generators, laser systems, and precision measurement equipment.

Research Equipment

X-ray diffractometer, scanning electron microscope, atomic force microscope, and cryogenic systems.

Computational Resources

High-performance computing cluster with MATLAB, Mathematica, COMSOL, and physics simulation software.

Observatory

On-campus telescope facility with CCD cameras and spectrographs for astronomical observations and research.

Capstone Design Examples

Quantum Entanglement Experiment

Design and build apparatus to demonstrate quantum entanglement using photon polarization measurements.

Solar Cell Efficiency Optimization

Investigate materials and design parameters to improve photovoltaic cell performance and efficiency.

Gravitational Wave Data Analysis

Develop algorithms to detect and characterize gravitational wave signals from LIGO data.

Plasma Confinement Simulation

Create computational models to study magnetic confinement in fusion plasma systems.
