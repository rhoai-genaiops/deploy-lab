Bachelor of Science in Economics

4 Years, 240 ECTS

A quantitative economics program combining economic theory, mathematical modeling, and data analysis to understand markets, policy, and global economic systems.

📄 Download Program Info Sheet (PDF)

Program Overview

Award: B.Sc. in Economics
Duration: 8 Semesters (4 academic years)
Total Credits: 240 ECTS
Delivery: Lectures (L), Tutorials (T), Laboratories (P), Studio/Project (S), Fieldwork (F)
Workload: 1 ECTS ≈ 25–30 hours

Program Pillars

  • Microeconomic Theory
  • Macroeconomic Theory
  • Econometrics
  • Mathematical Economics
  • Public Policy
  • International Economics
  • Financial Economics
  • Behavioral Economics
  • Applied Research

Graduate Learning Outcomes

Graduates will be able to:

1

Economic Analysis

Apply economic theories and models to analyze market behavior, policy impacts, and economic phenomena.

2

Quantitative Methods

Use econometric techniques and statistical methods to test hypotheses and interpret economic data.

3

Policy Evaluation

Assess the effectiveness of economic policies and recommend evidence-based policy solutions.

4

Research Design

Design and conduct empirical research studies addressing important economic questions.

5

Global Understanding

Analyze international economic relationships and their impacts on domestic and global markets.

6

Critical Thinking

Evaluate economic arguments critically and identify assumptions underlying economic models.

7

Communication

Present economic analysis and research findings clearly to diverse audiences including policymakers.

8

Professional Application

Apply economic principles in business, government, and non-profit organizational contexts.

Laboratories & Facilities

Econometrics Laboratory

Computer lab with Stata, R, Python, and MATLAB for statistical analysis and economic modeling.

Financial Data Center

Real-time financial data terminals with Bloomberg, WRDS, and economic databases for research.

Behavioral Economics Lab

Experimental facilities for conducting controlled economic experiments and behavioral studies.

Policy Research Center

Collaborative space with policy databases, government data access, and presentation facilities.

Capstone Design Examples

Housing Market Analysis

Analyze local housing market trends and develop policy recommendations for affordable housing.

Climate Change Economics

Evaluate economic impacts of climate policies and carbon pricing mechanisms.

Labor Market Mobility Study

Investigate factors affecting worker mobility and employment transitions using panel data.

Cryptocurrency Market Analysis

Analyze volatility patterns and market efficiency in cryptocurrency markets.
