Bachelor of Science in Electrical Engineering

4 Years, 240 ECTS

A comprehensive electrical engineering program covering power systems, electronics, control systems, communications, and emerging technologies like renewable energy and IoT.

📄 Download Program Info Sheet (PDF)

Program Overview

Award: B.Sc. in Electrical Engineering
Duration: 8 Semesters (4 academic years)
Total Credits: 240 ECTS
Delivery: Lectures (L), Tutorials (T), Laboratories (P), Studio/Project (S), Fieldwork (F)
Workload: 1 ECTS ≈ 25–30 hours

Program Pillars

  • Circuit Analysis & Design
  • Electronics & Semiconductors
  • Power Systems & Energy
  • Control Systems
  • Communications
  • Signal Processing
  • Embedded Systems
  • RF & Microwave
  • Professional Practice

Graduate Learning Outcomes

Graduates will be able to:

1

Circuit Analysis

Analyze and design complex electrical circuits using fundamental laws and advanced techniques.

2

System Design

Design and implement electrical and electronic systems meeting specified requirements and constraints.

3

Laboratory Skills

Use modern instruments and tools to measure, test, and characterize electrical systems and components.

4

Problem Solving

Apply engineering principles and mathematical methods to solve complex electrical engineering problems.

5

Project Management

Plan, execute, and manage engineering projects within time, budget, and quality constraints.

6

Communication

Communicate technical concepts effectively through reports, presentations, and professional documentation.

7

Ethics & Safety

Apply professional ethics, safety standards, and regulatory requirements in engineering practice.

8

Lifelong Learning

Adapt to emerging technologies and continue professional development throughout their careers.

Curriculum Structure

Year 1

Semester 1 (30 ECTS)

  • Calculus I (Single Variable) - 6 ECTS
  • Physics I (Mechanics) - 6 ECTS
  • Introduction to Electrical Engineering - 6 ECTS
  • Programming for Engineers (C/C++) - 6 ECTS
  • Engineering Graphics & CAD - 6 ECTS

Semester 2 (30 ECTS)

  • Calculus II (Multivariable) - 6 ECTS
  • Physics II (Electricity & Magnetism) - 6 ECTS
  • DC Circuit Analysis - 6 ECTS
  • Digital Logic Design - 6 ECTS
  • Engineering Ethics & Communication - 6 ECTS

Year 2

Semester 3 (30 ECTS)

  • Differential Equations - 6 ECTS
  • AC Circuit Analysis - 6 ECTS
  • Electronic Devices & Circuits I - 6 ECTS
  • Signals & Systems - 6 ECTS
  • Technical Elective I - 6 ECTS

Semester 4 (30 ECTS)

  • Linear Algebra & Complex Analysis - 6 ECTS
  • Electronic Devices & Circuits II - 6 ECTS
  • Electromagnetic Fields - 6 ECTS
  • Circuit Analysis Laboratory - 6 ECTS
  • Technical Elective II - 6 ECTS

Year 3

Semester 5 (30 ECTS)

  • Control Systems I - 6 ECTS
  • Power Systems I (Generation & Transmission) - 6 ECTS
  • Digital Signal Processing - 6 ECTS
  • Electronics Laboratory - 6 ECTS
  • Technical Elective III - 6 ECTS

Semester 6 (30 ECTS)

  • Communication Systems - 6 ECTS
  • Power Electronics - 6 ECTS
  • Microprocessors & Embedded Systems - 6 ECTS
  • Technical Elective IV - 6 ECTS
  • Summer Industry Internship - 6 ECTS

Year 4

Semester 7 (30 ECTS)

  • Control Systems II (Digital Control) - 6 ECTS
  • RF & Microwave Engineering - 6 ECTS
  • Power Systems II (Protection & Stability) - 6 ECTS
  • Technical Elective V - 6 ECTS
  • Senior Design I (Planning & Design) - 6 ECTS

Semester 8 (30 ECTS)

  • Senior Design II (Implementation & Testing) - 12 ECTS
  • Engineering Management & Economics - 6 ECTS
  • Renewable Energy Systems - 6 ECTS
  • Professional Engineering Seminar - 6 ECTS

Laboratories & Facilities

Electronics Labs

Modern workbenches with oscilloscopes, function generators, spectrum analyzers, and component testing equipment.

Power Systems Lab

High-voltage equipment, protective relays, motor drives, and renewable energy simulation systems.

VLSI Design Lab

Cadence and Synopsys design tools, IC fabrication equipment, and semiconductor characterization systems.

Control Systems Lab

Real-time control systems, PLCs, robotic arms, and process control simulation environments.

Capstone Design Examples

Smart Grid Monitoring System

Design IoT-based monitoring and control system for distributed renewable energy sources.

Autonomous Drone Navigation

Develop embedded control systems for autonomous navigation and obstacle avoidance in drones.

Wireless Power Transfer

Create efficient wireless charging systems for electric vehicles and mobile devices.

5G Communication Module

Design and implement high-frequency communication modules for next-generation wireless systems.
