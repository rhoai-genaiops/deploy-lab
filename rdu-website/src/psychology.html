Bachelor of Science in Psychology

4 Years, 240 ECTS

A scientific approach to understanding human behavior, cognition, and mental processes through research, theory, and practical application.

📄 Download Program Info Sheet (PDF)

Program Overview

Award: B.Sc. in Psychology
Duration: 8 Semesters (4 academic years)
Total Credits: 240 ECTS
Delivery: Lectures (L), Tutorials (T), Laboratories (P), Studio/Project (S), Fieldwork (F)
Workload: 1 ECTS ≈ 25–30 hours

Program Pillars

  • Research Methods
  • Cognitive Psychology
  • Developmental Psychology
  • Social Psychology
  • Clinical Psychology
  • Neuropsychology
  • Statistics & Analysis
  • Ethics & Practice
  • Applied Psychology

Graduate Learning Outcomes

Graduates will be able to:

1

Research Design

Design and conduct empirical research studies using appropriate methodological approaches and ethical guidelines.

2

Statistical Analysis

Apply statistical methods to analyze psychological data and interpret research findings accurately.

3

Theoretical Understanding

Demonstrate comprehensive knowledge of major psychological theories and their applications.

4

Assessment & Evaluation

Administer and interpret psychological assessments and evaluation instruments appropriately.

5

Cultural Competence

Understand and respect cultural diversity in psychological research and practice.

6

Professional Communication

Communicate psychological concepts effectively to professional and lay audiences.

7

Ethical Practice

Apply ethical principles and professional standards in all psychological activities.

8

Critical Thinking

Evaluate psychological research critically and apply evidence-based approaches to problem-solving.

Laboratories & Facilities

Behavioral Research Labs

Observation rooms with one-way mirrors, video recording equipment, and behavioral coding software.

Cognitive Testing Center

Standardized testing environments with psychological assessment tools and computerized testing systems.

EEG/Psychophysiology Lab

Brain imaging equipment, eye-tracking systems, and physiological monitoring devices for research.

Statistics & Data Lab

Computer lab with SPSS, R, and other statistical software for data analysis and research projects.

Capstone Design Examples

Mental Health App Development

Design and test a mobile application for anxiety management using evidence-based psychological interventions.

Workplace Stress Intervention

Develop and evaluate stress reduction programs for improving employee mental health and productivity.

Educational Psychology Research

Investigate learning strategies and their effectiveness in improving academic performance in diverse populations.

Community Mental Health Program

Create and implement community-based mental health initiatives addressing local population needs.
