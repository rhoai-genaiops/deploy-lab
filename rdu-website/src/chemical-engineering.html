Graduate Learning Outcomes

Graduates will be able to:

1

Process Design

Design and optimize chemical processes for manufacturing, considering safety, economics, and environmental impact.

2

Transport Phenomena

Apply principles of heat, mass, and momentum transfer to solve engineering problems.

3

Reaction Engineering

Design and analyze chemical reactors for optimal conversion and selectivity.

4

Process Control

Implement control systems for maintaining safe and efficient process operations.

5

Safety & Risk Assessment

Identify hazards and implement safety management systems for chemical processes.

6

Problem Solving

Apply engineering principles and computational tools to solve complex chemical engineering problems.

7

Sustainability

Develop environmentally sustainable processes and technologies for green manufacturing.

8

Professional Practice

Work effectively in teams and communicate technical concepts to diverse audiences.

Laboratories & Facilities

Unit Operations Lab

Distillation columns, heat exchangers, pumps, and process equipment for hands-on learning.

Process Control Lab

DCS systems, PLCs, and instrumentation for process automation and control studies.

Reaction Engineering Lab

Various reactor types including batch, CSTR, and packed bed reactors for kinetic studies.

Simulation & Computing

ASPEN Plus, COMSOL, and MATLAB for process modeling and design optimization.

Capstone Design Examples

Sustainable Biofuel Production

Design integrated process for converting biomass to biofuels with minimal environmental impact.

Pharmaceutical Manufacturing Plant

Develop complete process design for API production including safety and regulatory compliance.

Carbon Capture Technology

Design novel CO2 capture and utilization processes for industrial emissions reduction.

Water Treatment Optimization

Optimize membrane-based water treatment processes for industrial wastewater management.
