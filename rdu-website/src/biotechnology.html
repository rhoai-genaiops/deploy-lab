Bachelor of Science in Biotechnology

4 Years, 240 ECTS

A lab-intensive, industry-aligned biotechnology curriculum bridging molecular & cell biology, chemistry, bioinformatics, bioprocess engineering, and regulatory affairs.

📄 Download Program Info Sheet (PDF)

Program Overview

Award: B.Sc. in Biotechnology
Duration: 8 Semesters (4 academic years)
Total Credits: 240 ECTS
Delivery: Lectures (L), Tutorials (T), Laboratories (P), Studio/Project (S), Fieldwork (F)
Workload: 1 ECTS ≈ 25–30 hours

Program Pillars

  • Molecular & Cell Biology
  • Biochemistry & Microbiology
  • Genetics & Genomics
  • Bioinformatics & Data
  • Analytical Techniques
  • Bioprocess & Downstream
  • Quality & Regulatory
  • Bioethics & Society
  • Entrepreneurship

Graduate Learning Outcomes

Graduates will be able to:

1

Molecular Analysis

Apply molecular, cellular, and biochemical principles to design experiments and interpret data.

2

Laboratory Techniques

Operate core lab techniques (PCR/qPCR, cloning, protein expression/purification, chromatography, spectroscopy, microscopy, flow cytometry) with GLP/GMP awareness.

3

Data Analysis

Model & analyze biological data using statistics, R/Python, and bioinformatics tools (NGS pipelines, sequence analysis).

4

Bioprocess Design

Design & run bioprocesses from bench to pilot scale; perform downstream processing and quality testing.

5

Safety & Ethics

Assess biosafety, biosecurity, and ethical/regulatory implications of biotech applications.

6

Communication

Communicate scientific findings effectively; collaborate in multidisciplinary teams.

7

Translation

Translate discoveries into products via IP, regulatory strategy, and business planning.

Laboratories & Facilities

Core Teaching Labs

BSL-1/2 spaces; cell culture suites (biosafety cabinets, CO₂ incubators); microbial labs; analytical rooms.

Equipment

PCR/qPCR, gel systems, spectrophotometers/plate readers, HPLC/UPLC, GC, FPLC/AKTA, centrifuges, fermenters/bioreactors (1–10 L bench + pilot), flow cytometer, confocal microscope.

Computational

Linux workstations, HPC access, R/Python, Galaxy, Git, ELN/LIMS.

Quality

Reference materials, SOPs, clean areas, environmental monitoring tools.

Capstone Design Examples

Engineered Enzyme for Plastic Degradation

Design and optimize enzymes for breaking down plastic waste using directed evolution and computational modeling.

CHO Process Optimization with PAT

Develop process analytical technology for real-time monitoring and control of Chinese Hamster Ovary cell culture.

CRISPR Diagnostic Platform

Create a rapid, point-of-care diagnostic system using CRISPR technology for infectious disease detection.

Microbial Consortia for Wastewater

Engineer microbial communities for enhanced removal of pollutants from industrial wastewater.
