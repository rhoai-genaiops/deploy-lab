Graduate Learning Outcomes

Graduates will be able to:

1

Structural Design

Design safe and efficient structures using principles of mechanics, materials science, and building codes.

2

Project Management

Plan, execute, and manage complex civil engineering projects within time, budget, and quality constraints.

3

Infrastructure Systems

Analyze and design infrastructure systems including transportation, water supply, and waste management.

4

Geotechnical Analysis

Assess soil and rock properties to design foundations and earthworks for construction projects.

5

Environmental Solutions

Develop sustainable engineering solutions that protect and enhance environmental quality.

6

Professional Practice

Apply engineering ethics, safety standards, and regulatory requirements in professional practice.

7

Technology Integration

Utilize modern engineering software, BIM, and digital tools for design and analysis.

8

Community Impact

Design infrastructure that serves community needs while considering social and economic factors.

Laboratories & Facilities

Structures Laboratory

Universal testing machines, concrete mixing equipment, and structural loading frames for materials testing.

Geotechnical Laboratory

Soil testing equipment, triaxial chambers, and consolidation apparatus for foundation analysis.

Hydraulics Laboratory

Open channel flumes, pipe networks, and flow measurement devices for water engineering studies.

CAD & BIM Labs

AutoCAD, Revit, SAP2000, and ETABS software for structural design and building information modeling.
